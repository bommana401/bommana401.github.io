Advantages of Solar Energy:

Types of Solar Panels:


Monocrystalline:

Advatage: High efficiency and performance
Disadvatage: Higher cost
Efficiency: 15-22%
Type of Silicon: Single Crystal of Silicon
Life Span: Over 25 years
Power Output Degrades: 0.8%
Type of Cell: Black cells

Polycrystalline:

Advatage: Low cost
Disadvatage: Less efficiency and performance
Efficiency: 15-17%
Type of Silicon: Fragments of crystals that are melted together into a mold.
Life Span: over 25 years
Power Output Degrades: 0.8%
Type of Cell: Blue cells

Thin-film:

"Best to install on large and commercial roof that can’t handle the additional weight of traditional solar equipment. Thin-film panels can be a useful for portable solar systems, like on RVs or boats."

Advatage: Portable and fLexible
Disadvatage: Lower efficiency and performance
Efficiency: 11%
Type of Silicon: Single Crystal of Silicon
Life Span: Less than 25 years
Power Output Degrades: 0.2% to 4% (depending on the material type)
Type of Cell:
  • Amorphous silicon (a-Si)
  • Cadmium telluride (CdTe)
  • Copper Indium Gallium Selenide (CIGS)

What Should You Consider Before Installing Solar Systems for Residential Roofs:

Source: EnergySage

  • Rooftops may not be suitable for solar systems due to tree cover.
  • The size, slope, and material of your roof are also important factors to consider. It is difficult to install solar systems on roofs made from wood and slate.
    • Steep roof - it may not be possible to place panels at the optimal tilt with traditional racking systems. It is best to lay your panels flat against the roof.
    • Low-angle roofs - will also face obstacles when it comes to solar panel installation and may require specialized racking if you’re looking to tilt them at the optimal angle. Flat roofs often produce less electricity.
    • Flat roof - solar installers will usually opt to use racking systems that mount your panels up at an optimal angle. Flat roofs limit the system size. Tilting panels cause panels to shade one another unless you space and stagger the rows of panels out on the roof.
  • The age of your roof might prevent you from installing solar system.
  • The amount of weight your roof can hold can affect the size of your solar system. The roofs on most newer homes can handle significantly more than 3 pounds per square foot of weight.

    To calculate to see if you can roof can handle a solar system, look at the example below: In a 6 kilowatt solar installation made up of 20 solar panels, the panels alone will weigh approximately 800 pounds (20 panels x 40 lbs). Based on our calculation above, we know that this same system measures at 352 square feet, which works out to a weight of 2.3 pounds per square foot (800 pounds ÷ 352 sq ft). When you include the mounting hardware and other equipment for your panels, the total weight will be closer to 3-4 pounds per square foot.”

    Average Weight and Size of Panels:

    Each panel has approx. 60 cells and weighs between 33 - 50 pounds. Most weigh about 40 pounds. Newer roofs can handle significantly more than 3 pounds per square foot of weight. Panel measurements are 65 inches by 39 inches.

    • The ideal Placement for solar Panels on a Roof:

  • The ideal angle for a solar panel installation is ideally the latitude of your home (on a south-facing roof), somewhere between 30 degrees and 45 degrees. By tilting your solar panels close to the angle of latitude faces the panels perpendicular to the sun. Ths way, you ensure maximum average output from your solar power system.

Definitions & Information :

  • Efficiency:
  • Efficiency is the percentage of the solar energy shining on a PV device that is converted into usable electricity.
  • Temperature coefficient:
  • A solar panel’s temperature coefficient measures how much or little its production drops in hot temperatures.
  • Power Output:
  • Power output refers to the amount of electricity a panel generates.

Solar power storage:

“Storage” refers to capturing electricity, store it as another form of energy and then releasing it for use. Lithium-ion batteries allow for energy storage. Although energy storage is never 100% efficient, storage allows the flexible use of energy. Storage can increase system efficiency and resilience, while improving quality by matching supply and demand.
