Biogas, what is it for?

From "Dalkia"

Advantages of Biogas Energy:

  • According to EESI reduces needless food waste, organic wastes are numerous and extend to non-edible sources, including livestock manure, agriculture wastes, wastewater, and inedible food wastes.
  • EESI states that fossil fuels are finite. Bioenergy/biogas is an alternative to fossil fuels and can often be produced infinitely as long as we have biological waste (food and organic waste).
  • Often food waste that goes into land waste produces methane gas due to anaerobic decomposition. Methane gas is really bad for the environment. Therefore, when this biogas is used for daily applications, it is an infinite source of energy unlike
  • Department of Energy states "When fossil fuels are burned, they release carbon dioxide and other gases into the atmosphere. Some of these gases pollute the air we breathe and contribute to climate change – which threatens ecosystems and could lead to flooding, drought, or famine in some parts of the world.”
  • NREL states that biogass reduces the green house emissions. NREL explains that Even though biomass produces the same amount of fossil fuel, CO2 emitted from fossil fuels is considered a “new” greenhouse gas. Biomass, on the other hand, releases carbon dioxide that is largely balanced by the carbon dioxide captured in its own growth (depending on how much energy was used to grow, harvest, and process the fuel).

Biodigestor:

Biodigestors take in organic waste such as human food waste and/or excreta along with anume excreta to produce biogass and a liquid fertilizer. Liquid fertilizer which can be used to produce nutrients and rich produce while increasing soil’s productivity. According CTC-N "A biodigester consists of an airtight, high-density polyethylene container within which excreta diluted in water flow continuously and are fermented by microorganisms present in the waste. The fermentation process is anaerobic, i.e., it takes place without oxygen, and the bacteria responsible for decomposition are methanogenic (i.e., they produce methane, also known as biogas). Processed manure is an organic, pathogen-free fertilizer that is rich in nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium. The products are primarily for self-consumption on farms. "

Limitations to biogas:

Source: CTCN

    Organic Waste:

  • Need about 6 pounds of food waste in the digestor to make gas. Usually the amount of organic waste varies based on the digestor. Read instrcutions to know what quantities should be used.
  • It is important not to exceed the maximum recommended organic matter capacity.

    • Biodigesters:

  • The biodigester must be used constantly; otherwise, organic waste starts to rot in the container.
  • Biodigesters can be selective about what kinds of organics wastes can be put in them.
  • Must maintain a certain distance between appliances that use biogas and the biodigester.

    • Weather:

  • Climate needs to be in a warmer climate (at least 59°F) and encouraged to be placed under the sun. During cold weather, the digestor needs to be placed in a greenhouse or thermal insulation.

    • Leakage:

  • Though leakage of methane gas is rare, methane gas is dangerous because its highly combustible. If you suspect a leak, keep everyone away from the area and let the surrounding people know to avoid ignition sources. Proper ventilation should help. Contact emergency services.
